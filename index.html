Discovering the Ecuadorian Sierra

Introduction

The Ecuadorian Sierra is a region that extends from the north to the south of the Andes Mountains. This region is one of the most visited regions out of the four with a wide natural and cultural diversity. It is the most distinguished because of its volcanoes, thermal waters, lakes, national parks, and most importantly its colonial cities, towns and architecture. It is conformed by 10 provinces, each with similar traditions but distinct characteristics that make them unique.

The Provinces

Azuay Province

The Azuay province was created in June 25, 1824 and has been considered a meeting point for the cultures of La Costa and La Amazonia regions because of its geographical location. It contains one of the most important cities of Ecuador and also the provinces capital: Cuenca. The city of Cuenca has been declared as a world heritage by UNESCO because of its preserved neoclassical colonial architecture, vestige archeology, artistic productions, literature and other intangible characteristics. Cañar Province

The province of Cañar houses the most archeological sites that can be found in Ecuador, reflecting the deeper history of Ecuador and its people. It is because of these sites, rich in history that the province was named the “Archaeological Capital of Ecuador”. The capital of El Cañar is the canton of Azogues which is the largest and most populous city of this province and the most developed. There are 6 other cantons which include Biblian, Cañar, Deleg, El Tambo, La Troncal and Suscal. The canton of Cañar gives the province its name and its a site of historic interest as it was inhabited by the native indians of that time before colonization. Carchi Province

The Carchi province is located in the north of Ecuador bordering Colombia. This province is described as the Golden Door of Ecuador because of its landscape and the richness of its land. The province got its name by the river that divides Ecuador from Colombia, the Carchi River. A fun fact about the name Carchi is that it means the border or limit of an area. The capital of the Carchi Province is called Tulcan. Tulcan offers a variety of flora and fauna along with a 38km elevation in the center of the canton. Another 5 cantons conform this province including Mira, San Pedro de Huaca, Bolivar, Espejo y Montúfar. Mira is characterized by its museums, enriched by the presence of a religious cult of the region. The canton of Huaca is home to the religious culture in El Carchi with many monuments dedicated to the Virgin Mary. It is a very visited and sacred place of Ecuador containing various sanctuaries. Chimborazo Province

The Chimborazo Province is rich in culture and folklore, one that is recognized all over the country. It holds a strong religious belief and medicine that is very developed for the province. The province is a majestic landscape as it is surrounded by three snowy mountains which highlight the peak where the sun and earth meet, the Chimborazo peak. This province is perfect for those who love mountain sports like climbing. It is made up of 10 cantons which include: Riobamba, Alausí, Chambo, Chunchi, Colta, Cumandá, Guamote, Guano, Pallatanga and Penipe. Imbabura Province

The Imbabura province is widely known as the Lake province for its immense quantity of lakes along the provinces limits. Around 420k people inhabit this province in all its 6 regions, with Atuntaqui being the largest. Atuntaqui holds the most famous textile factory and most productive of the province helping with its economy. The other cantons are: Cotacachi, Ibarra, Otavalo, Pimampiro and Urcuqui. Ibarra is described as the White City which to this day contains the signs of invasions and the lives of the people. It holds a colonial architecture which has been mixed with modern ones. Tungurahua Province

The province of Tungurahua is also known as the land of the Three Juanes for being the birthplace Juan Montalvo, Juan León Mera, and Juan Benigno Vela. All three Juans are important in Ecuadorian literature and history. Its biggest attractions are its craftsmanship and plaza’s along with the historic places that inhabit it. The streets are filled with the floral decorations and even exude a more vibrant environment during the festivals. The province gained its name because of the volcano found in it that is also called Tungurahua. Its capital is the city of Ambato which contains the statues in honor of the 3 Juanes.
